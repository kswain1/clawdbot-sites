The Story — 5th Grade Edition

Imagine Gold is a Rubber Band 🏅

🎯

Step 1 — Aura Watches the Rubber Band

Every 5 minutes, Aura checks how stretched or compressed the gold price rubber band is. It draws a normal "calm zone" in the middle and two lines at the edges — the Upper Wall and the Lower Floor. These are called Bollinger Bands. When the price gets too close to the walls, something usually happens.

🔍

Step 2 — Is the Market Bouncing or Running?

Before deciding what to do, Aura asks: "Is gold in a calm, bouncy mood — or is it sprinting in one direction?"
It uses three sensors:

ADX — Trend Strength ATR — Volatility Size ROC — Speed of Move

If all three say "GO GO GO" → TREND MODE. If they're all calm → CONSOLIDATION MODE. If mixed signals → TRANSITION (sit out).

⚡

Step 3 — TREND Mode (Sprint Race)

When gold is running fast in one direction and it breaks through a wall, Aura jumps in immediately — no waiting. Like catching a wave. If gold breaks the Upper Wall while sprinting UP, we ride it up. If it crashes through the Floor while sprinting DOWN, we ride it down. We risk 20 points, aiming for 56 points back (2.8× reward).

⏳

Step 4 — CONSOLIDATION Mode (Rubber Band Snap)

When gold is calm and bouncing around, Aura watches for it to touch the walls. When it does, it usually snaps back to the middle. But instead of jumping in right away (which can be a fake-out), Aura waits 20 minutes to confirm the signal is still valid. If it is — we enter. Risk 20 points, target 56 points back.

🛡️

Step 5 — Safety Rules

Every trade has a Stop Loss (a maximum pain point — we exit if price goes against us 20 points). We also limit how much we lose per day (4% of the account = $400 max daily loss). If we hit that limit, the bot pauses for the rest of the day.

📢

Step 6 — The Discord Alerts

In TREND mode: one immediate alert fires — enter now.
In CONSOLIDATION mode: a 5-step countdown fires over 20 minutes (20 min → 15 → 10 → 5 → ENTER NOW → 15-second final window). This gives you time to get to your terminal, check the chart, and place the order confidently.
